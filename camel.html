Interesting facts about camels:

  • Camels' humps don't store water; they store fat which can be converted to food or water when needed.
  • They're very social animals. In the wild, they often travel with about 30 others while looking for food.
  • A camel might appear to have thick fur, but its coat reflects the sun, which helps keep them cool in the desert.
  • Baby camels don't have hump, but it appears once they are old enough to eat solid foods.
  • Camels can run up to about 65 kilometers per hour when needed and can maintain a steady pace of 40 kilometers per hour.
